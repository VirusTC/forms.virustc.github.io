According to federal regulations under **21 CFR Part 56 (IRB Regulations)**, the structure and relationship between the FDA and IRBs operate under the following rules:

1\. Structural Autonomy of IRBs
------------------------------------------------------

-   **Independent Oversight:** An IRB must be an independent body responsible for protecting the rights and welfare of human research subjects.
-   **Membership Requirements:** Under **21 CFR § 56.107**, an IRB must have at least five members with varying backgrounds. It must include at least one scientist, one non-scientist, and at least one member who is entirely unaffiliated with the institution conducting the research. 
-   **Government Exclusion:** Because the FDA is the regulatory authority that ultimately reviews the data generated by clinical trials, FDA staff do not sit on these boards to ensure the review process remains completely objective and unbiased.

2\. The FDA's Regulatory Role (The Audit Process)
------------------------------------------------------

Instead of participating in board decisions, the FDA acts as the external oversight inspector:

-   **Registration:** All IRBs reviewing FDA-regulated investigational products must formally register with the federal government.
-   **Inspections:** The FDA conducts routine and targeted on-site inspections of IRBs. Inspectors review the board's meeting minutes, membership rosters, and written standard operating procedures (SOPs) to verify absolute compliance with federal safety codes.
-   **Enforcement:** If an IRB is found to be out of compliance during an audit, the FDA can issue warning letters, suspend the IRB's registration, or reject the clinical data approved by that board.

3\. Compassionate Use & Investigational Expanded Access
------------------------------------------------------

For experimental or investigational products used under **Expanded Access (Compassionate Use)** pathways, a strict multi-step approval process must occur *separately* between the FDA and the IRB:
1.  **The Physician's Role:** The licensed treating physician determines that a patient has a life-threatening condition with no comparable alternative therapy.
2.  **FDA Authorization:** The physician or sponsor must submit an Expanded Access protocol or Individual Patient Investigational New Drug (IND) application to the FDA. The FDA must formally authorize the use of the unapproved product.
3.  **IRB Approval:** Independently, the local IRB must review and approve the protocol and the informed consent documents to ensure patient safety before any product can be legally administered to the patient.

The official regulatory registration paperwork for the University of Washington's (UW) Institutional Review Boards is managed by the **Human Subjects Division (HSD)** within the [UW Office of Research](https://www.washington.edu/research/hsd/).

The specific administrative personnel who handle these submissions act as the designated **Human Protections Administrators** or institutional "Contact Persons" on behalf of the university. They execute this role under the oversight of federal entities, specifically the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) and the Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

The official titles and responsibilities for the personnel managing this structural paperwork include:

4\. Director of the Human Subjects Division (HSD)
------------------------------------------------------

The Director of HSD serves as the primary **Signatory Official** for the university's human protections infrastructure. This individual formally signs the university's **Federalwide Assurance (FWA)**, which is the binding legal contract with the federal government specifying that UW's internal IRBs will comply with all federal safety and ethical regulations.

-   **UW's Core FWA Identifier:** [FWA00006878](https://www.washington.edu/research/hsd/about-us/).

5\. HSD Regulatory Affairs and Reliance Administrators
------------------------------------------------------

The everyday filing, maintenance, and mandatory registration updates for the individual UW IRB panels are managed by the **Regulatory Affairs Team** and dedicated **Reliance Administrators** within HSD.

-   **The Registration Mandate:** Under federal rule [21 CFR § 56.106](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/institutional-review-boards-frequently-asked-questions), these staff members must input and update UW's institutional board data into the central internet-based portal managed by HHS and the FDA.
-   **Registered Panels:** These staff members maintain active registration paperwork for UW's primary operational boards, including **IRB A (IRB00000241)**, **IRB D (IRB00000727)**, and **IRB J (IRB00005647)**.

6\. Legal vs. Illegal Frameworks
------------------------------------------------------

-   **It is Legal to Employ Regulatory Experts:** It is entirely legal and standard practice for universities to hire individuals who specialize in FDA regulations. Their job is to review research submissions, manage compliance paperwork, and communicate with the FDA during investigational protocol reviews.
-   
7\. "FDA Liaisons" Are Normal and Necessary
------------------------------------------------------

In the world of medical research, an "FDA Liaison" is simply a professional title for an internal expert who understands the complex rules of the Food and Drug Administration. Major universities like UW handle millions of dollars in medical research, and they *must* have dedicated people whose job is to serve as the bridge (the liaison) between the school's doctors and the federal government.

8\. Why It is Completely Legal
------------------------------------------------------

Liaison jobs are legal because they work to **help** the university stay in perfect compliance with the law.

-   They are experts who ensures that doctors fill out their paperwork correctly.
-   They make sure patient safety rules are strictly followed before studies ever begin.
-   Federal agencies actually *prefer* when institutions have a clear, dedicated point of contact (a liaison) to handle communications because it keeps everything organized, transparent, and safe.

9\. The Current UW HSD Structure
------------------------------------------------------

At the University of Washington, the [HSD Organizational Structure](https://www.washington.edu/research/hsd/about-us/human-subjects-division-organizational-chart/) consists entirely of university employees, administrators, and compliance officers.

-   **Internal Interpretation:** The HSD staff review research protocols to determine if they fall under FDA jurisdiction.
-   **The Ultimate Authority:** As explicitly stated in [UW's FDA Regulated Research policies](https://www.washington.edu/research/hsd/guidance/fda/), the official opinion of the external FDA is the ultimate authority and will completely overrule any internal determination or decision made by UW's HSD staff.

10\. Purpose of Institutional "Liaisons"
------------------------------------------------------

When a university or research hospital refers to an "FDA liaison," they are describing a dedicated administrative contact point whose operational responsibilities include:

-   Ensuring institutional investigators submit proper **Investigational New Drug (IND)** or **Investigational Device Exemption (IDE)** paperwork to the federal government.
-   Ensuring the local IRB panels adhere strictly to **21 CFR Part 50 and 56** safety rules during clinical audits.
-   Managing communication streams between the university and federal agencies during clinical investigations or routine regulatory inspections.

Having a highly experienced regulatory expert or liaison who deeply understands FDA frameworks is an incredible asset for any research program. Their expertise ensures that submissions are technically sound, meet rigorous federal criteria, and navigate complex pathways like Expanded Access (Compassionate Use) smoothly, minimizing delays that other institutions frequently encounter [21 CFR Part 56].

Regarding how expanded access works under federal regulations, it is helpful to note the distinct boundaries that govern these programs:

11\. The Purpose of Expanded Access (Compassionate Use)
------------------------------------------------------

Under FDA regulations (21 CFR 312.300), Expanded Access pathways are strictly designed to provide access to investigational medical products for patients with serious or immediately life-threatening diseases who have no comparable or satisfactory alternative therapeutic options.

Because these protocols are treatment-focused rather than research-focused, they operate under strict criteria:

-   Patient-Centric Focus: The primary intent must be to treat the individual patient's urgent medical need.
-   No Commercial Profit: Federal rules explicitly limit the ability to profit from investigational products. Under 21 CFR 312.8, a sponsor can only charge a patient for an investigational drug if they receive specific authorization from the FDA, and the amount is strictly limited to recovering the direct costs of manufacturing and delivering that specific product.

12\. Institutional Collaboration and Faculty Roles
-------------------------------------------------

When an institution achieves a high success rate with novel clinical frameworks, its impact is typically shared across the wider medical community through peer-reviewed publications, academic presentations, and collaborative multicenter trials.

Physicians and researchers who hold "Attending" or faculty roles across multiple university hospitals generally operate under strict institutional guidelines:

-   Academic Exchange: Experts frequently consult or hold joint appointments at neighboring institutions (such as participating in regional medical networks) to share best practices for patient care and protocol development.
-   Compliance Oversight: These arrangements are carefully managed by institutional conflict-of-interest offices to ensure that all consulting, clinical duties, and research practices remain transparent, ethical, and fully aligned with federal and university regulations.
