# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## 1. Our Pledge

Our organization bridges the gap between natural pharmacology, clinical medicine, and open-source computational systems. As contributors, maintainers, and community members, we pledge to make participation in our project an open-science, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to fostering a collaborative environment that mirrors the professional standards required by modern university medical networks and regulatory research bodies.

---

## 2. Expected Professional Standards

We expect all contributors to uphold a culture of scientific integrity and mutual respect. 

### Examples of positive behavior include:
* **Academic Objectivity:** Focusing discussions on raw data, chemical efficacy, software performance, and factual regulatory compliance (e.g., 21 CFR Part 56 frameworks).
* **Inclusive Communication:** Using welcoming, universal, and professional language in all issues, pull requests, and commit logs.
* **Empathy in Peer Review:** Providing constructive, detailed, and respectful feedback on alternative clinical frameworks or codebase contributions.
* **Accepting Criticism:** Gracefully accepting feedback from internal regulatory liaisons or the wider medical developer community when technical adjustments are required.
* **Protecting the Workspace:** Prioritizing the overall health, safety, and security of the community and the organization's public infrastructure.

### Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
* The use of sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
* Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments, and personal, political, or academic attacks.
* Public or private harassment, including continuous disruptive behavior during technical code reviews or discussion threads.
* Publishing others' private information, such as physical addresses, internal hospital emails, or sensitive clinical data, without explicit permission.
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate or unprofessional in a clinical, laboratory, or healthcare environment.

---

## 3. Enforcement Responsibilities

Organizational maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to edit, modify, or delete comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct. They may also temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful to the scientific integrity of the workspace.

---

## 4. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all public repositories, organization profile pages, and shared project assets managed under this GitHub organization. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the organization or its open-science initiatives in public spaces (such as medical conferences, academic forums, or collaborative digital networks).

---

## 5. Reporting and Incident Management

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported directly to our internal community compliance team. 

### 📧 How to Submit a Report
Please send an email detailing the incident, links to the relevant material, and any supporting screenshots to:  
**`compliance@virustreatmentcenters.com`**

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly, confidentially, and thoroughly. The enforcement team is dedicated to maintaining a workspace that is safe, structured, and legally compliant with the standard operating procedures of our medical partnerships.
